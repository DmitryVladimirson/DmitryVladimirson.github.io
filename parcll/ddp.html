DDP versus DDU.

DDU is the traditional postal model: Delivered Duty Unpaid.

Typically when sending a package from the United States under DDU terms, the USPS will be the go-to provider. The USPS will arrange your package all the way up to the border of your destination country, where the destination postal office will take over.

The destination postal organization (for example the French Post in France, Germany Post in Germany etc.) will arrange for postal clearance and depending on the package value will contact the consumer to pay for import VAT and possibly Duty.

In addition to VAT and Duty, the postal organization in the destination country will also charge a customs clearance handling fee to the consumer, which typically amounts to USD 8-12 in local currency.

DDP is the modern commercial model: Delivered Duty Paid.

When sending a package cross-border under DDP terms, the shipper will pay for destination import VAT and possibly duty. With Parcll, your package will be moved from the United States to destination on a commercial flight and Parcll will arrange for fully electronic DDP clearance for Ecommerce at destination. VAT and possibly duty will be assessed upfront and are guaranteed.

Also, Parcll does not charge any customs clearance fees to shipper or consumer.

The DDP model avoids any unforeseen charges to shipper or consumer. After DDP customs clearance, Parcll arranges delivery through its commercial delivery network, contracting the best in country providers (courier, parcel or postal) to ensure smooth delivery experience for consumers.
