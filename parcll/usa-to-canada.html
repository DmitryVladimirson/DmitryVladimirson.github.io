Shipping Challenges in Canada

By total area (including its waters), Canada is the second-largest country in the world, however Canada ranks only 38th by population with over 37 million Canadians as of 2019. The vast majority of the country is sparsely inhabited, with most of its population south of the 55th parallel north. More than half of Canadians live in just two provinces: Ontario and Quebec. Though Canada’s population density is low, many regions in the south such as the Quebec City–Windsor Corridor, have population densities higher than several European countries.

Reaching the sparsely inhabited regions and remote areas in Canada is a logistical challenge. Parcll cooperates directly with Canada Post in order to reach each and every consumer in the country.

FAQ

Can I add insurance to cover my deliveries to Canada?

Yes. Parcll services include basic insurance with every delivery. Additional high-value insurance is available for purchase.

What happens in a package cannot be delivered in Canada?

If your package can’t be delivered, it will be returned to the Parcll warehouse to be scanned and sorted. From there, it can be sent for delivery again or returned to the original shipper.

What items cannot be shipped to Canada?

Any obscene materials, coins, cash, counterfeit goods, or anything that includes sedition, hate speech, or misrepresentation of their place of origin are prohibited from being sent to Canada.
